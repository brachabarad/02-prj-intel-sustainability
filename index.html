Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

Intel's Sustainability Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel releases the world's first microprocessor, the 4004, revolutionizing computing technology.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance.

1993

Pentium Processor

Intel launches the Pentium processor, setting a new standard for performance in personal computing.

2006

Core Processor

Intel introduces the Core processor family, delivering improved performance and energy efficiency.

2011

3D Transistors

Intel unveils 3D transistor technology, revolutionizing chip design and boosting performance.

2017

10nm Processors

Intel begins production of 10nm processors, advancing semiconductor manufacturing technology.

2022

Sustainability Goals

Intel pledges to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040, reinforcing its commitment to sustainability.

Sustainability Goals

RISE Strategy

Under its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy, Intel sets ambitious 2030 goals, including driving industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Learn More

Commitment

In 2022, Intel pledged to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This commitment builds on decades of environmental initiatives and partnerships across the semiconductor industry.

Learn More

Water & Waste

Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually and collaborates with local communities to restore watersheds. Intel also upcycles and recycles materials to reduce waste and advance a circular economy.

Learn More

Innovation

Intel continues to innovate in energy-efficient technologies, ensuring that sustainability is at the core of its product design and manufacturing processes.
